# Thread 3: Retroactive Public Goods Funding

1/12
Most grants ask: "What will you build?"

Retroactive public goods funding asks: "What did you already make better?"

That one change matters because public goods impact is often easier to judge
after the work exists.

2/12
Optimism helped push retro funding into the crypto mainstream.

The basic thesis: if builders believe valuable impact will be rewarded later,
they can take risks today without needing every detail approved up front.

Fund impact, not promises.

3/12
Retro funding is powerful because it can reward work that proposal systems miss:

- unglamorous maintenance
- docs that reduce support load
- security research
- governance operations
- data tooling
- ecosystem education
- dependency upgrades

Impact is often discovered late.

4/12
It also changes builder incentives.

Instead of optimizing for grant applications, teams can optimize for measurable
ecosystem value.

That is healthier when the funders do not know in advance which experiments
will actually matter.

5/12
But retro funding has a big challenge: measurement.

Who decides what counted?
Which metrics matter?
How do you avoid rewarding only visible work?
How do you prevent professional claim-writing from beating quiet maintenance?

The oracle problem is social, not only technical.

6/12
Badgeholder systems are one answer.

Give evaluation power to a group selected for judgment, context, and reputation.

That introduces human review, but it can also introduce bias, fatigue, and
coordination costs.

Evaluation design becomes part of the protocol.

7/12
Retro funding also needs clean scopes.

"Fund everything good" is too vague.

Better scopes sound like:

- developer tooling for a specific ecosystem
- governance research used by delegates
- infrastructure that reduced downtime
- education that onboarded measurable users

8/12
The strongest retro claims are evidence-rich.

They show:

- what shipped
- who used it
- what changed because of it
- why the work was non-excludable or ecosystem-wide
- why normal revenue did not fully capture its value

Impact needs a paper trail.

9/12
Retro funding also pairs well with forward grants.

Forward grants can fund exploration.
Retro funding can reward outcomes.

Together, they reduce the risk of both underfunding early work and overfunding
promises that never become useful.

10/12
The future is likely more modular:

- open impact data
- better contribution graphs
- public attestations
- domain-specific reviewers
- transparent scoring rubrics
- appeals
- anti-gaming checks
- repeatable evaluation cycles

Retro funding is becoming infrastructure.

11/12
The key lesson:

Public goods funding is not only about generosity.

It is about creating credible expectations that useful work for the commons can
be rewarded, even when no single customer owns the benefit.

12/12
CTA:

If you build public goods, start an impact log now.

Write down users helped, issues fixed, dependencies maintained, risks reduced,
and decisions influenced.

Retro funding rewards evidence. Do not wait until the round opens.
